## Research Question
Is there a significant difference in mean height among students with different levels of milk drinking frequency?

## Variables

### Explanatory Variable (Group Variable) and Response Variable
• Explanatory Variable (Group Variable): NoMilkDrinking  
• Response Variable: HowTallAreYouWithoutShoesInMeters  

## Method Used
A one-way ANOVA was used to test for differences in mean height across milk drinking frequency groups. When a statistically significant result was found, a Tukey HSD post-hoc test was conducted to identify which specific groups differed from each other.

## Final Results

• Reject H₀. There is sufficient statistical evidence at the 0.05 significance level to conclude that at least one group mean height is  
  significantly different among the three milk drinking groups.

• Post Hoc (Tukey HSD) results showed the following pairwise mean differences in height:
  1. No milk drinking vs. Occasional milk drinking = 0.0104  
  2. No milk drinking vs. Frequent milk drinking = 0.0364  
  3. Occasional milk drinking vs. Frequent milk drinking = 0.0259  

## Final Conclusion

The ANOVA result indicates that at least one group mean differs significantly, but it does not identify which specific groups differ. Tukey HSD post-hoc tests revealed significant differences across all pairwise comparisons. Descriptive statistics further suggest a trend where frequent milk drinkers have the highest mean height, followed by occasional milk drinkers, and then non-drinkers.
